Stir-Fried Ginger Broccoli Beef

Description

Ginger broccoli beef is a delectable stir-fry dish that combines tender strips of beef with crisp broccoli florets, all bathed in a savory ginger-infused sauce. This dish is a harmonious fusion of flavors and textures that has become a favorite in both Chinese and Western cuisines. The key to its irresistible appeal lies in the perfect balance of ingredients.

Beyond its delicious taste, ginger broccoli beef also boasts health benefits. Ginger is renowned for its anti-inflammatory properties and digestive aids, making it a therapeutic addition to the dish. Meanwhile, broccoli is a nutritional powerhouse, packed with vitamins, fiber, and antioxidants. When combined with lean beef, it provides a balanced meal that is both indulgent and nutritious. Whether served over a bed of steamed rice or alongside noodles, ginger broccoli beef is a versatile and crowd-pleasing option, suitable for both weeknight dinners and special occasions. Its enticing aroma and the perfect interplay of flavors ensure that this dish remains a beloved classic in the world of culinary delights.

Ingredients

  • 7 ounces beef sirloin, sliced into strips
  • 1 Â¼ teaspoons chicken bouillon granules, divided
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar, divided
  • 1 tablespoon ginger juice
  • 3 tablespoons water, divided
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 (4 inch) piece ginger root, peeled and sliced
  • 14 ounces Chinese broccoli, sliced

  1. Step 1: Toss beef strips with 1 teaspoon chicken bouillon granules and 3/4 teaspoon sugar in a bowl. Marinate in the refrigerator, 20 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Step 2: Combine remaining 1/4 teaspoon chicken bouillon granules, 1/4 teaspoon sugar, and ginger juice in a bowl.
  3. Step 3: Mix 2 tablespoons water with cornstarch in a small bowl. Add to bowl with ginger juice mixture; stir.
  4. Step 4: Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a wok over medium-high heat; cook and stir ginger until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add broccoli; cook and stir for 2 minutes. Add remaining 1 tablespoon water, cover, and cook until broccoli is cooked but still crunchy, about 1 minute. Transfer to a dish.
  5. Step 5: Heat remaining 1 tablespoon oil in the wok. Add beef; cook and stir to medium well doneness, about 4 minutes. Add ginger juice mixture and broccoli. Cook and stir until heated through, about 2 minutes.
